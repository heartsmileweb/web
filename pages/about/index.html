Our Story

HeartSmile Meditation was introduced to the United States at the International Symposium for Contemplative Studies (ISCS) 2016 at the University of California, San Diego.

Developed by Venerable Misan Sunim and the HeartSmile Meditation Research Institute, this practice combines traditional Buddhist wisdom with modern psychological understanding to create a meditation approach that is accessible and effective for contemporary practitioners.

Our Philosophy

The foundation of HeartSmile Meditation is accepting the body and mind as they are, embracing yourself as you are right now. This practice helps cultivate compassion for all beings by first developing deep self-acceptance.

Through the meditation language "I am whole as I am right now," practitioners can temporarily suspend discriminative thinking and experience a profound sense of wholeness and acceptance.

Our Approach

HeartSmile Meditation offers a structured yet flexible approach to mindfulness and compassion practice. Our programs range from introductory workshops to advanced training for dedicated practitioners.

We emphasize both formal meditation practice and the integration of mindfulness and compassion into daily life, helping practitioners develop a sustainable and transformative practice.
